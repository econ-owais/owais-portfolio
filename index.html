About Me

I chose economics because I wanted to understand how systems work — how decisions ripple through communities, how policies shape lives, and how data can tell stories that matter. It wasn't just theory for me; I wanted to use it to fix things, build smarter tools, and make public services actually serve.

Along the way, I picked up tech skills that helped me turn ideas into action. With R, Stata, and Shiny, I started building dashboards, mapping flood risks, and analyzing budgets for central banks and government teams. I've worked on everything from welfare planning to transport systems — always focused on clarity, impact, and real-world use.

I care about making things simple, sharp, and accessible. Whether it's a policy report or a public-facing app, I push every detail until it works for everyone. Outside the data world, I've led student teams, mentored others, and helped communities during floods. For me, it's all connected — economics, tech, design, and outreach.

This is more than a career. It's how I build things that matter.
